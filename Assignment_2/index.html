Regional Areas Remains Shaky

Since 2014, Northern Territory has had the highest suicide rate, peaking at 21.8 in 2014. Although, it has shown signs of decline alongside its neighbouring states (WA, TAS, QLD, SA) since 2020. Interestingly, Australian Capital Territory had halved its suicide rate to 7.7 in 2023 from its peak in 2017.

Indigenous Disparities

A significant discrepancy exists in suicide rates between Indigenous and non-Indigenous Australians. The Indigenous Australians experienced higher rates of suicide in its peak of 30.8 in 2023, whereas non-Indigenous Australians had its lowest rate of 11.1 in 2023, urging immediate call for action to improve indigenous mental health support.

Socioeconomic Disparities

Notable disparity in suicide rates can be seen between the top 20% and bottom 20%. Individuals from the lowest socioeconomic quintile experienced a decline in mental health outcomes, resulting in a suicide rate of 18.2 in 2023 compared to 12.5 in 2018. Conversely, suicide rate for the top 20% declined to 7.5 in 2023 from 10.3 in 2013.
