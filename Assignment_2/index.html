Australian Mental Health Datascape

According to Australian Institute of Health and Welfare, 1 in 5 Australians aged 16-85 experienced a mental health disorder. The numbers don't lie, as do the real stories behind the statistics. Thus, we aim to shed light on the issues that are plaguing the nation.

Regional Areas Remain Shaky

Since 2014, Northern Territory has had the highest suicide rate, peaking at 21.8 in 2014. Although, it has shown signs of decline alongside its neighbouring states (WA, TAS, QLD, SA) since 2020. Interestingly, Australian Capital Territory has halved its suicide rate to 7.7 in 2023 from its peak in 2017.

Source: Hospitalisations for intentional self-harm 2023–24 – National Hospital Morbidity Database , Australian Institute of Health & Welfare (26 August 2025).

Self-Harm Crisis Among Youth

A concerning trend of young people aged 0-24, followed by those aged 25-44, highlights the most vulnerable age groups in this Australian mental health predicament. Northern Territory, Tasmania, Queensland, Australian Capital Territory were particularly affected deeply, averaging roughly 200 in self-harm hospitalisations across age group between 0-24 and 25-44 for both genders.

Worrying Signs of Female Self-Harm

Across all states, female age groups have shown a disturbing pattern of self-harm hospitalisations. Northern Territory, Queensland, Tasmania, South Australia, and Australian Capital Territory consistently reached beyond 250 in 2015-19 and 2020-22, particularly among young female aged 0-24 and 25-44, outpacing their male counterparts across the age groups.

Australian Indigenous Gap

A significant discrepancy exists in suicide rates between Indigenous and non-Indigenous Australians. The Indigenous Australians experienced higher rates of suicide in its peak of 30.8 in 2023, whereas non-Indigenous Australians had its lowest rate of 11.1 in 2023, urging immediate call for action to improve indigenous mental health support.

Great Socioeconomic Divide

Notable disparity in suicide rates can be seen between the top 20% and bottom 20%. Individuals from the lowest socioeconomic group experienced significant jump in suicide rate of 18.2 in 2023 compared to 12.5 in 2010. Conversely, suicide rate for the top 20% declined to 7.5 in 2023 from 10.3 in 2010, highlighting the growing divide in the socioeconomic status.

Desperate Plea for Help

According to World Health Organisation, 1 in 8 people are struggling with mental health issues globally. Hence, countless people flock to online forums like Reddit to express their mental struggle. Impactful words such as, 'Feel', 'Help', 'Know', 'Life', and 'People' are quite obvious, along with negative terms such as 'Depression' and 'Anxiety'.

If you or someone you know is struggling with mental health issues, please reach out to a trusted friend, family member, or mental health professional. Remember, you are not alone.
